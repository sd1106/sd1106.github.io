The "Other 6%"

During my time building a highly accurate IT support chatbot, I realized something critical: building systems that work 94% of the time isn't enough. It's about what happens in the margins—the edge cases, the biases, the unexpected human elements.

This perspective was deeply shaped by my younger brother, Shreshth, who has experienced speech and cognitive delays since undergoing brain surgery at age one. For years, I sat beside him through dozens of exercises and rigorous therapy programs, and learned that progress is anything but linear. It requires patience, constant iteration, and tools that mold to the user, not the other way around. I don't just want to build things that mostly work; I want to understand why they fail, fix them, and design human-centered technology that genuinely cares.
